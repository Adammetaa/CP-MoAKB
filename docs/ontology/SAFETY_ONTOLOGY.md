# Safety Ontology

## Purpose and authority boundary

The Safety Ontology defines concepts needed to keep hazard, exposure, assessed risk, protective controls, label instructions, and legal restrictions separate. It does not provide safety conclusions, emergency instructions, product permissions, or application recommendations.

Safety and legal conclusions MUST rely on authoritative, current, jurisdiction-specific sources appropriate to the exact product, label version, use pattern, population, place, and time. General scientific material or another jurisdiction cannot establish a local legal requirement.

## Core concepts

| Concept | Meaning and important relationships |
| --- | --- |
| Hazard | An intrinsic potential to cause harm under a stated classification system, endpoint, source, and version. Hazard alone is not assessed risk. |
| Exposure | Contact between an agent and receptor, with route, magnitude, duration/frequency, timing, activity, method, and uncertainty. |
| Risk | An assessment combining a defined hazard and exposure scenario for an endpoint/population, using a named method and assumptions. |
| RouteOfExposure | A governed route relevant to an exposure assessment; it does not imply that exposure occurred. |
| Operator | A person conducting mixing, loading, application, cleaning, transport, disposal, or related work. |
| Bystander | A person potentially exposed without participating in the operation. |
| Consumer | A person considered in dietary/residue risk scope. |
| NonTargetOrganism | An organism or population not intended as the treatment target, with ecological and exposure context. |
| EnvironmentalCompartment | Air, water, soil, sediment, crop, or other compartment within a defined environmental model. |
| ProtectiveMeasure | A control intended to reduce exposure or consequence, with source, applicability, limitations, and required/proposed status. |
| PPE | Personal protective equipment specified by an authoritative label, rule, standard, or reviewed guidance for a defined task. |
| RestrictedEntryInterval | A source-stated time restriction after application, tied to exact product/use, jurisdiction, effective label, and exceptions. |
| PreHarvestInterval | A source-stated minimum interval tied to exact product/crop/use, jurisdiction, and effective label. |
| ApplicationConstraint | A limit concerning method, rate/use pattern, timing, weather, buffer, equipment, frequency, area, or other condition. |
| LabelInstruction | Versioned text or structured assertion from an authoritative product label, with section/locator and effective status. |
| LegalRestriction | A binding jurisdictional constraint with authority, legal basis, scope, and validity period. |
| EmergencyInformation | Authoritative, versioned response/contact information for a defined product/event/jurisdiction; currency is critical. |
| Jurisdiction | The legal/geographic scope and responsible authority for a regulatory or label statement. |

## Required distinctions

- **Intrinsic hazard** characterizes harmful potential under a classification scope.
- **Exposure scenario** describes who/what may contact what, how, how much, and when.
- **Assessed risk** is a method- and assumption-dependent conclusion for a hazard/exposure combination.
- **Label requirement** is a versioned instruction on an authoritative product label.
- **Legal requirement** arises from a competent authority and legal instrument; not every best practice is law.
- **Best-practice guidance** is sourced guidance whose authority, scope, and review status remain explicit.
- **Recommendation** is a later context-specific decision output constrained by all applicable safety and legal requirements.

A product registration does not prove low hazard, absence of exposure, efficacy, or suitability. A PPE statement MUST identify whether it is label-required, legally required, or guidance. PHI and REI MUST NOT be inferred, generalized across products/crops, or carried forward after a label changes.

## Relationships and constraints

- Hazard **is classified for** an agent/product under a named system and source version.
- ExposureScenario **involves** a receptor, route, activity, product/agent, environment, duration, magnitude, and controls.
- RiskAssessment **evaluates** hazard and exposure using a named method, assumptions, uncertainty, date, and reviewer.
- SafetyRequirement **constrains** application; the source identifies whether it is label, legal, or other authoritative guidance.
- Legal permission **depends on** exact product, crop, target, use pattern, jurisdiction, label/registration state, and time.
- EmergencyInformation **applies to** a defined product/event and MUST expose currency and authoritative contact/source.

Conflicting or superseded safety sources MUST remain auditable and MUST block an unqualified conclusion until resolved by the responsible authority or review process. Missing current jurisdictional evidence requires deferral, not invention.
