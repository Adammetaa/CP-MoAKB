# Thai Rice Department Source Assessment

## Assessment scope

This bounded review was performed on 2026-07-13 against official `ricethailand.go.th` resources. It sampled the Rice Knowledge Bank landing page, topic indexes, a fact-sheet index, and one official PDF. It did not crawl the site, download source files, copy images, assess every child page, or establish completeness.

The reviewed pages identify the Rice Department as publisher and the Rice Research and Development Division/Information and Communication Technology Center in the site footer. The visible Knowledge Bank platform label is “version 4 (256802)”; this is site-level metadata and MUST NOT be treated as the publication date or revision of every child page.

## Architectural findings

- Coverage exists for crop/production knowledge, disease, insect and other animal pests, weeds, symptoms/signs where described, damage, stage, organ, and production context, but coverage completeness was not measured.
- Topic lists, fact sheets, and PDFs likely overlap. Thai/common/scientific terminology and category placement may vary; matching labels or shared links do not prove equivalence.
- Current portal pages may preserve older material without page-level dates. Archived pages and apparently superseded publications require additional currency review.
- Structured paraphrase may be suitable only after source-unit, rights, domain, and provenance review. Long quotations and source images are outside the pilot plan.
- Scientific names require specialist taxonomic verification. Rice Department content does not automatically establish taxonomic equivalence.
- Rice Department content may support Thai rice-domain knowledge, but it does not replace IRAC, FRAC, or HRAC classification authorities.
- Technical pest-management guidance is not automatically current product authorization. Regulatory facts and recommendations remain separate, jurisdiction- and time-bound datasets.

## Required review before extraction

For each selected child page/publication: verify official publisher and stable reference; capture publication/revision and retrieval metadata; determine current/archived state; assess reuse/quotation/image rights; define the claim-scoped extraction unit; separate description from guidance; verify scientific identity externally; review disease/pest/weed/stage/organ meaning; record duplication/conflicts; and obtain evidence, regulatory-separation, architecture, and domain decisions.
