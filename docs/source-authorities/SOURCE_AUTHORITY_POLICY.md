# Source Authority Policy

## Claim-scoped authority

CP-MoAKB selects sources per claim type, jurisdiction, and time. Authority is not a universal source ranking. A national crop authority may govern Thai production terminology while a taxonomic authority governs organism identity, an international committee governs its classification, and a current regulator governs legal use.

## Source categories

| Category | Typical claim scope and caution |
| --- | --- |
| International classification authority | Versioned classification it owns; not taxonomy, local regulation, diagnosis, or efficacy. |
| National government authority | Mandated national subject matter and guidance; confirm mandate, date, and jurisdiction per claim. |
| Scientific taxonomic authority | Taxon identity, rank, nomenclature, and history within its coverage/version; not pest role or causality. |
| Peer-reviewed scientific literature | Study-supported claims in stated methods/populations; not automatic national policy. |
| University or research institution | Specialist research/extension content; authority depends on review and claim scope. |
| Manufacturer label | Product-owned label assertions for a named market/version; not independent scientific or registration authority. |
| Regulatory registration source | Current legal registration/status in its jurisdiction and effective period; not general agronomic truth. |
| Extension publication | Interpreted practical guidance; verify currency, source basis, and local applicability. |
| Reviewed field evidence | Contextual evidence promoted under governance; not automatically generalizable. |
| Unreviewed field observation | Private evidence candidate only; never canonical fact or public authority. |

## Required distinctions

The source organization, source document/record, source-owned version, publication date, retrieval date, subject-domain authority, jurisdiction, temporal validity, evidence quality, extraction method, review status, and copyright/reuse status are separate metadata. Technical access does not imply reuse permission.

Conflicts are represented at statement level. Reviewers compare claim relevance, source mandate, methods, version/currency, jurisdiction, specificity, traceability, and contrary evidence; they do not silently choose the most official-looking domain.
