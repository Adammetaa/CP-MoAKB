# Master Data Principles

Future CP-MoAKB master datasets MUST follow these principles:

1. **Governed identity:** one identity represents one intended reference entity within a declared scope. Identity is stable independently of preferred labels.
2. **Source-backed content:** every material descriptive field and relationship is ready for statement-level provenance; a source is authoritative only for the relevant claim.
3. **No silent identity change:** suspected duplicates are reviewed. Merges and splits preserve history, mappings, and affected releases. Identifiers are never reused.
4. **Language separation:** Thai, English, scientific names, synonyms, scripts, and transliterations are governed language forms, not identity keys. Multilingual absence is explicit.
5. **Context:** temporal validity, jurisdiction, production context, evidence quality, review status, and uncertainty are represented where relevant.
6. **Layer separation:** canonical entities are separate from operational observations; scientific facts from regulatory facts; and descriptive data from diagnosis and recommendation logic.
7. **Bounded claims:** every dataset declares scope, exclusions, known gaps, and the precise meaning of any completeness claim.
8. **Reproducible curation:** source versions, retrieval, extraction method, transformations, reviewer decisions, and conflicts are auditable.
9. **Non-destructive history:** corrections, deprecations, source withdrawals, and contradictory evidence remain traceable across releases.
10. **Controlled publication:** private or restricted material is not made public merely because a derived candidate exists.

These principles do not guarantee truth. They make uncertainty, authority, change, and review inspectable.
