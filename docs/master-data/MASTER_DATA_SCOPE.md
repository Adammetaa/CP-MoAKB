# Master Data Scope

## Conceptual domains

| Domain | Intended scope |
| --- | --- |
| Crop; Crop Taxon; Crop Variety or Cultivar | Governed crop concepts, scientific mappings, and named cultivated forms without representing planted instances. |
| Growth Stage; Plant Organ | Crop-specific stage systems and plant structures needed for descriptive relationships. |
| Disease; Disease Complex | Governed disease concepts, including explicitly reviewed multi-cause complexes. |
| Causal Agent; Pathogen Taxon | A causal role/assertion and a separately governed biological identity. |
| Insect or Arthropod Pest; Other Animal Pest | Organisms acting as pests in a stated crop and context, separated by domain-review needs. |
| Weed; Weed Taxon | A contextual weed role and a separately governed taxonomic identity. |
| Symptom; Sign; Observable Characteristic | Host responses, directly observable causal-agent evidence, and reusable observation descriptors. |
| Damage Mechanism; Damage Pattern; Feeding Guild | How injury occurs, what pattern it produces, and a reviewed behavioral classification. |
| Habitat or Production Context; Environmental or Predisposing Factor | Contexts and factors that qualify statements without being diagnoses. |
| Host Relationship; Affected Growth Stage; Affected Plant Organ | Contextual, evidence-bearing relationships rather than universal attributes. |
| Source; Evidence; Jurisdiction; Review Status | Provenance, support, applicability, and governance metadata. |

## Layer distinctions

- A **canonical entity** is a stable governed reference subject.
- A **classification category** organizes entities under a declared scheme and version.
- A **statement or relationship** asserts a connection and may require evidence, context, time, and confidence.
- **Source evidence** supports or contradicts a statement; it is not the statement itself.
- A **field observation** records an event or condition at a place and time; it is not master data.
- A **diagnosis** is an interpretation of evidence and uncertainty.
- A **recommendation** is a decision output gated by evidence, regulation, safety, and context.

The scope excludes products, registrations, treatments, legal-use conclusions, private field records, and complete domain coverage until separately governed.
