## Publication and rights identity

The reviewed source is Uwe Meier (editor), *Growth stages of mono- and dicotyledonous plants: BBCH Monograph*, Julius Kühn-Institut, Quedlinburg, 2018, DOI `10.5073/20180906-074619`, ISBN `978-3-95547-071-5`. The official English PDF is linked from <https://www.julius-kuehn.de/en/jki-publication-series/bbch-scale/p>.

Rendered PDF page 2 records the title, editor, publisher, DOI, ISBN, and CC BY 4.0 notice. PDF page 3 states that the scientific content is identical to the 1997 edition and identifies JKI as rightsholder. The rice key is section 1.3.2, “Phenological growth stages and BBCH-identification keys of rice (*Oryza sativa* L.), Lancashire et al., 1991,” on printed/PDF pages 23–27.

## Selected principal stages

Labels below were read directly from the rendered official pages. Boundary notes are concise original paraphrases of the displayed secondary-stage range; they are not copied definitions.

| Code | Official English label | Page | Treatment | Concise boundary/inclusion note | Ambiguity and secondary-stage relationship |
| --- | --- | ---: | --- | --- | --- |
| BBCH 0 | Germination | 23 | Direct label | Covers the progression from dry caryopsis through imbibition and root/coleoptile emergence to emergence of the still-rolled imperfect leaf. | The water-rice note changes the order of two secondary events; principal identity is unaffected. |
| BBCH 1 | Leaf development | 23 | Direct label | Counts unfolded leaves, beginning with visibility/unrolling of the first true leaf and continuing through nine or more leaves. | The page notes that tillering or stem elongation can begin before the leaf sequence finishes; stages may overlap operationally. |
| BBCH 2 | Tillering | 24 | Direct label | Runs from first detectable tiller through increasing tiller counts to the maximum detectable number. | If stem elongation begins first, the key directs use of stage 30 rather than forcing tillering to finish. |
| BBCH 3 | Stem elongation | 24 | Direct label | Begins with panicle initiation/green-ring development, continues through panicle formation and internode elongation, and ends around unfolded flag-leaf/pre-boot condition. | The principal label is broader than everyday “stem gets taller”; panicle initiation is included. |
| BBCH 4 | Booting | 25 | Direct label | Covers early, middle, and late boot conditions as the upper stem/flag-leaf sheath changes, ending when the flag-leaf sheath is open. | `Booting` is retained as the framework term; no local-stage equivalence is asserted. |
| BBCH 5 | Inflorescence emergence, heading | 25 | Direct label; punctuation retained | Tracks panicle emergence from first visible tip through increasing emergence to the end of panicle emergence. | The page notes flowering may start before the midpoint of stage 5; stages 5 and 6 can overlap. |
| BBCH 6 | Flowering, anthesis | 26 | Direct label; punctuation retained | Begins when anthers first become visible, includes full flowering, and ends after all spikelets complete flowering. | Completion can leave dehydrated anthers visible; anther presence alone does not identify the exact secondary stage. |
| BBCH 8 | Ripening | 27 | Direct label | Covers dough/ripening conditions from early dough through soft and hard dough to fully ripe grain. | Principal stage 7 lies between 6 and 8 in the complete scale and is deliberately omitted only from this pilot subset. |

## Visual and reuse controls

The reviewed rice pages contain a two-column code/description table and footnotes, not crop diagrams. The repository may record the publication identity, page locators, principal codes, concise principal labels, and original boundary paraphrases with attribution under CC BY 4.0. It must not reproduce the complete table, page layout, footnotes verbatim, detailed secondary-stage descriptions, title graphic, or any source image.

The visual review closes the source-inspection blocker for the selected principal-stage labels and boundary interpretation. It does not establish Thai mappings, equivalence to IRRI or Thai Rice Department stages, or scientific approval by an unassigned Agricultural Domain Reviewer.
