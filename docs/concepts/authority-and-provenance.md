# Authority and Provenance

Authority identifies who is competent or official for a defined scope; it is not
a universal truth flag. A source reference identifies material and relevant
metadata. Provenance records creation, source/evidence links, reviews, and
supersession without hiding transformations.

Labels, external identifiers, and repeated sources do not automatically establish
identity or equivalence. Consumers must preserve the scope and lifecycle attached
to authority and provenance. ADR-001, ADR-006, and RAS-004 provide the governing
direction.
