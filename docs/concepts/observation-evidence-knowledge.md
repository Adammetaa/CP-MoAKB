# Observation, Evidence, and Knowledge

An observation records what was seen or stated. Evidence connects a scoped claim
to an identified source and provenance. Knowledge requires governance of identity,
authority, interpretation, and lifecycle. Repetition alone does not promote an
observation to evidence or evidence to accepted knowledge.

Knowledge is not diagnosis; diagnosis is not recommendation; recommendation is
not decision; decision is not action; action is not outcome. Runtime records and
evidence primitives preserve these distinctions but do not perform scientific
promotion. ADR-001 and ADR-002 are the decision authorities.
